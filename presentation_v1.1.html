where the program started

I started where most operations people stop.

At the edge of what the data could tell me. The tracker existed. The dashboard existed. What didn't exist was anything that connected them to a question worth asking in real time.

So I built it. Not with a team, not with IT support, not with a budget. With a terminal, an AI collaborator, and a specific enough understanding of the operation that I could tell the model exactly where the gaps were.

04 · derived constants

Four constants govern the OR models.

κ · γ · ε · ρ — derived from 613 sorts
κ
Each zone has its own day-of-week appetite. Mondays in Zone 3 are not Wednesdays. κ tells the staffing optimizer what tonight will demand.
kappa · zone load
γ
PPH erodes through the sort week — not linearly. γ models the curve so forecasts don't assume Monday's pace on Friday.
gamma · weekly decay
ε
Scanner-ID substitution corrupts iGate PPH silently. ε quantifies the iGate ↔ SOR identity drift so fidelity calls are evidence-based.
epsilon · scan drift
ρ
Loaned sorters are a leading indicator of zone strain. ρ scores the density of borrow/loan events so DLI can pre-position labor.
rho · borrow risk
All four cross-validated across SOR/TMS and iGate/ESR independently.  Not estimated. Derived, validated, formalized.  Embedded as live parameters in the tracker's OR models.
